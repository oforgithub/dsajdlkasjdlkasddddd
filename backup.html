About Carlo A. Rolando

Carlo A. Rolando received his legal education at the University of Notre Dame, trained by one of Michigan’s most respected trial judges and by a host of Bay Area district attorneys. A Bay Area native, Mr. Rolando graduated magna cum laude from Saint Mary’s College of California before moving to Indiana to attend law school at the University of Notre Dame. In law school, Mr. Rolando excelled in written and oral advocacy and was selected by faculty members to represent Notre Dame at the American Bar Association’s National Appellate Advocacy Competition.

Following graduation, Mr. Rolando earned recognition as law clerk to the Chief Judge of Michigan’s 43rd Judicial Circuit Court. His experience and commitment to excellence have been recognized by various legal associations, including leadership capacities with the San Mateo County Bar Association and The American Bar Association.

Personal Injury Practice

Mr. Rolando's personal injury practice represents individuals and their families in wrongful death and personal injury cases stemming from motor vehicle negligence, product liability, premises liability, and construction accidents.
